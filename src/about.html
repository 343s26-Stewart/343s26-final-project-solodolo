About Dial

What is Dial?

Dial is a music discovery app that lets you explore artists, browse their discography, and keep track of who you want to listen to. It pulls live data from MusicBrainz, an open music encyclopedia maintained by the community.

How to use it

  1. 1
    Search — type any artist name on the Search page and hit the arrow button (or press Enter) to pull up matching results.
  2. 2
    Explore — click any result card to open the artist detail view. You'll see their genres and a full discography pulled from MusicBrainz.
  3. 3
    Save — add the artist to one or more collections, leave a personal note, and click Save to Collection. Head to My Collections to find them later.

Collections

Collections let you organize saved artists however you like. Dial starts you off with three — Want to Explore, Listening, and Listened — but you can create, rename, and delete collections freely from the My Collections page. An artist can belong to as many collections as you want.

Your collections and saved artists are stored locally in your browser. Use the Export button to back them up as a JSON file, and Import to restore them on another device.

Data

Artist search results and discography data come from the MusicBrainz Web Service, available under the CC BY-NC-SA 3.0 license. MusicBrainz is a community-maintained, open-source music metadata database. All collection data is stored only in your browser — nothing is sent to any server.
